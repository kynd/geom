<div class="prose">
<p>
  Rim intensity is {"\\( r = (1 - |\\, n \\cdot (-d) \\,|)^{p} \\)"} — bright at silhouette edges,
  dark face-on. The subsurface scattering (SSS) term fires a secondary ray through the shape's
  interior to measure thickness, then {"\\( e^{-\\,\\text{thickness} \\times \\text{density}} \\)"}
  lets thin regions glow from within. SSS is only applied to closed solid shapes (SDF shapes and
  Platonic duals); scalar and moving scalar fields are open surfaces with no enclosed interior, so
  there is no meaningful thickness to march through.
  The sliders expose rim exponent, face-on fill, SSS strength, and density.
</p>
</div>

  A precomputed environment map assigns every surface point a color based on which direction its normal faces.
